# Five Lanterns

**Five Lanterns** is a quiet, reflective application designed to support intentional self-observation through gentle prompts and ambient visual feedback.

Rather than aiming to diagnose, treat, or “fix,” Five Lanterns offers a calm space to notice patterns of thought, feeling, and behavior at your own pace. The experience is inspired by concepts often referred to as *shadow work*: sitting with experiences, asking careful questions, and allowing understanding to emerge without force.

## What It Is

Five Lanterns presents five lanterns, each representing a distinct area of reflection drawn from DSM-5–aligned symptom clusters:

- **Intrusive Thoughts**
- **Avoidance**
- **Cognition and Mood**
- **Compulsions**
- **Soothing and Substances**

Choosing a lantern reveals a single, thoughtfully written prompt. You may respond in writing, reflect silently, or skip the prompt entirely. Each interaction lights a portion of a stained-glass window. When five lanterns have been engaged, the window becomes fully illuminated, inviting a pause before beginning again.

There is no scoring, no time pressure, and no requirement to complete anything.

## What It Is *Not*

Five Lanterns is **not** a therapeutic tool, diagnostic system, or replacement for professional care.

- It does not provide treatment.
- It does not offer advice.
- It does not interpret responses.
- It does not claim to heal trauma.

Any journaling is private, local, and optional. Users remain fully responsible for their own interpretation and use of the experience.

## Design Philosophy

- **Gentle, not clinical**
- **User-led, not prescriptive**
- **Reflective, not corrective**
- **Ambient over gamified**

Visual elements—lantern light, subtle motion, stained glass—are meant to support presence without distraction. Progress is symbolic rather than evaluative.

## Ethical Boundaries

Five Lanterns is built with explicit ethical and regulatory boundaries in mind:

- Prompts are observational, not directive
- Language avoids diagnosis or instruction
- Users control pace, depth, and participation
- No data is collected, transmitted, or analyzed
- Journaling is entirely voluntary and is only saved to the local computer of the uesr.

This project is intended as a companion to reflection, not an authority over it.

## Status

Five Lanterns is under active development.  
Visual polish, prompt expansion, and accessibility improvements are ongoing.
